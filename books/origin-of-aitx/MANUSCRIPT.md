# The Origin of AITX

*A short illustrated comic to hand to sponsors and partners. Comic-book register (the locked AITX universe look). Narration lines are OVERLAID captions; art is textless except diegetic in-scene text (signs, the aitx logo, a Luma screen, the ANTLER step-and-repeat). No em dashes. Real subjects (Michael, Jake): GATED until they bless it.*

**Refrain:** You get a better sense of a person in person.
**Closing thesis:** If you want the right room of builders in Texas, there is a default answer, and it has open arms.

---

### Spread 0 (COVER, portrait)
Title: **The Origin of the AITX Community**. Subtitle: *How two builders turned ten people in a room into the largest AI builder community in Texas.*
*Art: Michael (mustache, low bun, AITX tee) and Jake (taller, short beard, AITX tee), warm and confident, the orange AITX open-arms mark above them. Baked cover title text.*

### Spread 1
SXSW, Austin, 2023. Two people were building with AI, and both had the same problem: nobody to actually talk to about it.
*Art: split scene at a loud South By night. Left: Michael filming a LangChain how-to on his phone, laptop open. Right: Jake at a Foundation Capital desk, building tools alone, screens glowing. Two islands in a crowded room.*

### Spread 2
Then they bumped into each other and had the conversation they had both been missing. So they made one decision: build the room they wished existed.
*Art: Michael and Jake mid-handshake and laugh at a South By party, the crowd blurred, a warm spark between them, the open-arms mark forming faintly like an idea.*

### Spread 3
April 2023, the first event. About ten people, off a Luma link, in a borrowed room. No venue of their own, no budget. Just two guys and whatever space they could get, in Austin.
*Art: a scrappy first meetup: about ten people on mismatched folding chairs in a plain borrowed room (NOT Antler, no branded wall), Michael and Jake at the front, a laptop showing a Luma page. Humble, warm, real, a little bare.*

### Spread 4
Ten became twenty-five, then a hundred. Borrowed rooms all over Austin, all filling up. Eventually Antler opened its doors and AITX had a home: a hub for engineers, operators, and really talented founders.
*Art: MULTI-PANEL, four panels left to right, the room filling and upgrading: 10 in a bare borrowed room, 25 in a nicer one, 100, then the packed Antler space with the ANTLER wood wall. The two founders constant at the front. Growth you can count.*

### Spread 5
It was never a room of sales pitches. It was builders showing real things. Ninety seconds each, live demos, the kind where somebody says here is a real problem, and here is what I made.
*Art: an AITX demo night: a builder presenting a live demo on the big screen to a rapt crowd, a countdown, other teams waiting their turn. Michael and Jake in the crowd, grinning. Diegetic: the aitx logo on the screen.*

### Spread 6
Then they stopped just talking and started building together. Hackathons. Moltathon. Web AI. The one with NVIDIA, where a team won on brand new hardware. Weekends that taught people the tools, put ideas in front of them, and got some of them jobs.
*Art: a lively AITX hackathon at Antler: people at laptops, whiteboards, sticky notes, a winning team celebrating, an NVIDIA-logo monitor. Late-night energy, collaboration. (Antler live/hackathon register.)*

### Spread 7
Reputation spreads, and it does not spread through sales. The sponsors came because these two did favors nobody asked them for and showed up when it did not pay. NVIDIA. Meta. Google Cloud. Each good thing made the next one easier.
*Art: the packed speaking alcove, the ANTLER + AITX step-and-repeat and NVIDIA monitor behind, a Meta Ray-Ban demo in the crowd, a warm full room. It compounds. (alcove-live; pass the AITX logo.)*

### Spread 8
Austin was never the whole point. They took it to Houston, then to Dallas, so the best Texas talent would have a reason to stay in Texas. A backstop of opportunity for local builders.
*Art: a map-lite montage or a Dallas/Houston kickoff room, new faces, the AITX open-arms mark planted in a new city, Michael opening the room. Texas, not just Austin.*

### Spread 9
Michael does not even own a car. He walks Austin, and when a new city needs him there, he rents one and drives up to host the event himself. He shows up. That is the entire strategy.
*Art: Michael on a Texas highway in a rented car, laptop bag on the seat, Austin skyline in the rearview and Dallas ahead. Determined, a little funny, generous. Small AITX mark on the bag.*

### Spread 10
Ask him to pitch AITX and he will not pitch AITX. He will tell you Texas is awesome, and that AITX is just the signpost pointing at it. That is why people trust it.
*Art: Michael gesturing warmly to a big AITX crowd, the open-arms mark like a signpost, a State of Texas AI report / survey visible on a table or screen. The community as a lighthouse for Texas AI.*

### Spread 11
Ask him why any of it works, and he points at the same thing every time. You get a better sense of a person in person. Loyalty. Patience. Kindness. Doing what you say. Keeping good company. Every honest interaction is one more piece of evidence, and evidence compounds into trust.
*Art: Michael mid-sentence on the lower-ground leather loveseat, warm and sincere, Jake beside him, the crowd leaning in. The human close-up of the whole ethos.*

### Spread 12
That is how ten people in a borrowed room became the largest AI builder community in Texas. Not with a campaign. With three years of showing up.
*Art: a wide, warm hero shot of a huge AITX crowd, hands raised, the orange open-arms mark large above them, Michael and Jake among the people. The payoff.*

### Spread 13 (the expectant, forward-looking beat)
And where is this going? AITX is becoming the room where Texas builders and the great tech companies meet. It is where people come to launch the future of their careers, and their lives.
*Art: a bright, hopeful, forward-looking scene. Michael and Jake look ahead toward a glowing sunrise over a Texas skyline (Austin, a hint of Dallas), the orange open-arms mark rising like a sun on the horizon, a diverse crowd of builders and tech-company people facing the same bright future. Optimistic, cinematic.*
