# AITX Brand Rules — visual identity

*The non-negotiables for how AITX looks. VOICE.md governs how we sound; this
governs the mark, logos, and co-branding. Read it before generating any visual
asset. A human can read the same file and hold the line.*

Last updated: 2026-07-19

---

## The mark

- The **orange open-arms mark** is the AITX logo. Always the real mark (passed
  as a reference on every render), never typed letters, never redrawn.
- Palette: orange `#ff4201`, black `#010101`, white `#ffffff`. Warm paper white
  `#fbf5ec` for grounds.

## Logos and co-branding (the cardinal rule)

**Mixing logos is almost never OK.** Do not combine, lock up, or place another
organization's logo next to the AITX mark. Do not render a partner's logo,
icon, wordmark, or official typeface at all. One asset carries **one mark, and
it is ours.**

A collaboration is expressed in **words and color, not logos:**

- **Words.** Name the partner in a sentence or headline, set in AITX's own type
  ("NVIDIA in the room", "with NVIDIA"). Plain text naming a partner is fine.
- **Color.** A small nod to a partner's brand color as an *accent* is fine
  (color is not a logo). Our orange still leads; the accent never co-leads.
- **Never** a "AITX × [partner]" logo lockup, a partner's eye-mark/wordmark, two
  logos side by side, or any composited emblem. If a piece would show a second
  logo, the design is wrong.

Why: a logo is someone else's trademark and equity. Combining marks implies an
official partnership we may not have, dilutes our mark, and reads as amateur.
Real brands reference partners in copy; they do not fuse logos.

## Approval

An asset is not **golden** until a human blesses it. Agent output is a
*candidate* (staged in `/explorations`), never written straight into
`/goldens`. See `goldens/README.md`.
