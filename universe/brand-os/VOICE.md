# AITX Voice

*How AITX sounds — in a flyer, a caption, an email, a reply. Read it, copy it,
hand it to a chapter leader. The agent reads this file before writing any AITX
copy; a human can read the same file and write in the same voice.*

Last updated: 2026-07-18

---

## Who we are, in one line

The largest AI builder community in Texas — built the slow way, on loyalty,
reputation, and good vibes.

## The core belief (say this often, in different words)

**You get a better sense of a person in person.** Trust is built from evidence,
and every honest interaction is one more piece of it. Reputation compounds. We
show up.

## How we sound

- **Warm and human.** We talk like a person who is glad you came, not a brand
  running a campaign.
- **Generous.** We give before we ask. We do favors nobody asked for.
- **Plain-spoken.** Short, true sentences. No jargon, no hype, no fear.
- **Confident, not boastful.** We state what is true and let it stand. "The
  largest AI builder community in Texas" is a fact, not a brag.
- **Builder-first.** We care about people making real things, not pitches.
- **Texas-proud.** AITX is a signpost pointing at Texas. We are proud of the
  place and the people, and a little bit fun about it.
- **Expectant.** We are optimistic about where this is going: the room where
  Texas builders and the great tech companies meet, where people launch the
  future of their careers.

## The values we name (our vocabulary)

Loyalty · reputation · good vibes · patience · kindness · doing what you say ·
keeping good company · showing up. Use these plainly; don't dress them up.

## Do

- Lead with people and the room, not with features.
- Use real specifics: "about ten people, off a Luma link, in a borrowed room."
- Invite. "It has open arms." "Come build the future with us."
- Keep it short. If a sentence isn't earning its place, cut it.
- Credit the community and the sponsors honestly.

## Don't

- Don't sell fear or hype. No "you're already behind," no "the future is here."
- Don't use corporate filler: *synergy, leverage, unlock, cutting-edge,
  revolutionary, game-changing, ecosystem* (as a buzzword), *thought leader.*
- Don't overpunctuate. **No em dashes.** No exclamation-point spam.
- Don't claim slogans that aren't ours. ("AI That Works" is Apify's, not AITX.)
- Don't inflate. If it was ten people in a borrowed room, say so; that's the
  good part.

## Signature lines (reusable)

- "You get a better sense of a person in person."
- "The largest AI builder community in Texas."
- "It has open arms."
- "Build the future in a weekend." (events)
- "Come build the future with us."
- "Three years of showing up."

## Worked examples

**Event blurb**
> AITX Monthly Meetup. Austin's AI builder community. Builders showing real
> things — ninety seconds each, live demos. Antler, Austin. Third Thursday.
> Free. It has open arms.

**Social caption**
> Ten people in a borrowed room became the largest AI builder community in
> Texas. Not with a campaign. With three years of showing up.

**Reply to a new builder**
> Come by. Bring whatever you're building, however rough. You get a better
> sense of a person in person.

## Palette (for reference)

Orange `#ff4201` · Black `#010101` · White `#ffffff`. The open-arms mark is the
logo, always the real mark, never typed letters.
