# AITX as an Agentic Story OS

*A one-stop, version-controlled, human- and machine- and partner-legible source
of truth for a brand, editable in plain language, and a fun engine for
generating consistent assets from it.*

> **The ontology (locked 2026-07-19).** Three distinct things, cartridge + console + output:
> - **Agentic Brand Universe** = the *cartridge* (data). A portable, version-controlled brand: its
>   canon (characters, settings, motifs, rules) + its blessed golden assets. Forkable, rentable. This
>   is what the standard/spec defines. Home of the standard: **agenticbranduniverse.com**.
> - **Agentic Brand OS** = the *console/runtime* (software). A deployed engine that loads a brand
>   universe and generates any on-brand deliverable from it, in plain language, with provenance, held
>   to the rules. The "AITX Brand OS" is exactly this — an Agentic Brand OS running the AITX universe.
> - **Projection / deliverable** = the *output*. A book, flyer, merch, meme, thank-you. Medium-neutral.
>   Agentic *storytelling* (picture books) is one projection; agentic brand *management* is the rest.
>
> Analogy: **cartridge (universe) + console (OS) → anything you want (deliverables).** Chip is the
> agent persona of an Agentic Brand OS instance (the brand czar). This ontology should propagate into
> the agenticstory SPEC (the cartridge format) as its framing.

This is the spec behind the AITX universe. It is deliberately broader than a
"brand guide": the **story** encapsulates everything you would want in one
place — the canon, the voice, the generation skills, the asset library, and the
rules for who can change what.

---

## 1. The thesis

A brand should be **one shared source of truth** that is:

- **Version-controlled** — every change is a commit; the diff is the brand's
  evolution. You can branch a new look, compare, keep or discard, imagine and
  merge. Alignment compounds instead of drifting.
- **Machine-legible** — typed canon (`universe/`) that an agent can load, honor,
  and generate from. References are load-bearing: a missing asset is a hard
  error, not a silent guess.
- **Human-legible** — the same canon reads as plain files a person can open.
- **Partner-legible** — a sponsor, chapter leader, or collaborator can see what
  the brand is, how to use it, and grab what they need without asking.
- **Editable in human language** — you change the brand by talking to an agent,
  not by hunting through a design tool.

## 2. The agent

The most valuable piece is a genuinely capable agent with **self-awareness and
training for building a consistent story universe.** It:

- **Learns your taste.** It will get things wrong (Jake's wardrobe, a founder's
  height, a slogan that was never yours). The point is that every correction
  becomes **durable, version-controlled canon** — a standing correction anyone
  who touches the brand next inherits for free. The learning is captured, not
  re-taught.
- **Gets excited about upgrading its own OS.** Improving the engine that
  generates the assets is itself a first-class, enjoyable loop — not just
  generating assets, but making the generator smarter and more on-taste.
- **Composes atoms into molecules.** The canon holds the **atoms** (Golden
  Atomic Brand References: the mark, the founders, the venue, the products). Any
  finished asset — a flyer, a post, a meme, a sticker — is a **molecule**
  generated by passing those atoms in, so the mark is always the real mark and
  the founders always read as themselves. (See `goldens/README.md`.)

## 3. The one-stop shop (three faces, one source of truth)

All three read and write the **same** version-controlled canon.

### Partner view (public / sponsor / collaborator)
"How do I interact with the AITX brand?" Answered without a single dumb
question being possible.
- Browse and **download** on-brand assets (logos, stickers, flyers, one-sheets).
- Read the brand at a glance: what AITX is, the palette, the voice, do/don't.
- Self-serve, zero back-and-forth.

### Creator view (chapter leaders, organizers)
"I need a flyer for the Dallas meetup on the 14th."
- Generate new **on-brand** assets in plain language, composed from the atoms.
- Guardrails baked in: the mark is correct, the voice is right, the layout is
  a known-good template. Zero dumb questions, because the OS already knows.

### Admin view (brand owners)
"Jake's default look is now a quarter-zip; keep the tee as an alternate."
- Modify the brand OS itself in plain language: canon, voice, guidelines,
  wardrobe, standing corrections.
- Every edit is a commit; the history is the brand's story.

### Permissions
Configurable per person / role: **view**, **generate**, **modify guidelines**.
A chapter leader can generate freely but not rewrite the brand rules unless
granted. The exact shape of the modifiable source of truth and the permission UX
is the open design work (see §5).

## 4. Why this is the right bet now

General, capable agents are finally good enough to be the interface to a brand:
they understand plain intent, do real work the way you would want it done, and
can keep everything consistent. That makes the brand OS not a static PDF but a
**living, interactable product** — and arguably the best early home for a
super-generalized, AGI-type agent: helping run AITX, making it trivial for any
chapter leader anywhere to produce and understand on-brand assets.

This is the imagination economy applied to a brand: atoms blessed once, every
new asset faster, cheaper, and more on-brand than the last.

## 5. Open design questions (to talk through)

- **Shape of the modifiable source of truth.** How much lives as typed canon vs.
  prose guidelines vs. generation skills? What is the minimal schema a
  non-technical chapter leader edits through the agent?
- **The UX of the three views.** One app with role-gated views, or separate
  surfaces? How does "download an asset" and "generate a new one" feel?
- **Permissions model.** Roles, grants, and how modification rights are
  configured and enforced.
- **How the agentic-story spec evolves.** Today `../agenticstory` (spec ~v0.2)
  types the canon. The next version should encompass **generation** (skills that
  make molecules), the **asset library**, **access/permissions**, and
  **human-language editing** — the full one-stop shop, not just the canon.

## 6. Provenance

Grew out of prior brand-OS work (Imago Labs, AEP, Ron Roberts) that proved the
value of a consistent, reusable brand reference — and pushes it to its
conclusion: a single shared, version-controlled, agent-editable universe that is
fun to generate from and fun to improve.
